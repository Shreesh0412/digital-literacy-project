# Case Study: Identity Theft

## Type of Cybercrime:
This case study focuses on a specific type of cybercrime known as identity theft.

## What is it:
Identity theft is a cybercrime in which a person’s personal or financial information is stolen and used without their permission. This may include details such as Aadhaar number, bank credentials, passwords, or social media accounts.

## How It Typically Happens (Step by Step):

- The attacker sends a fake email, message, or link pretending to be from a trusted source (bank, website, etc.).
- The message often creates urgency, such as asking the user to verify details or avoid account suspension.
- The user clicks on the link and is redirected to a fake website that looks real.
- The user enters personal information such as passwords, OTPs, or bank details.
- This information is captured by the attacker.
- The attacker then uses the stolen data to access accounts, make transactions, or misuse the identity.

## Who is Usually Targeted:

- Students and young internet users.
- People who are not fully aware of online security practices.
- Users who frequently share personal information online.
- Individuals who do not verify links or sources before clicking.
- People using multiple digital platforms without proper precautions.

## Consequences:

- Financial loss due to unauthorized transactions.
- Misuse of personal or social media accounts.
- Damage to personal or professional reputation.
- Loss of privacy and sensitive information.
- Difficulty in recovering hacked or misused accounts.

## Conclusion:

Identity theft is a serious cybercrime that can affect both financial and personal security. It highlights the importance of being careful while sharing personal information online. Staying informed and alert is essential to avoid becoming a victim.

#### Key Preventive Measures:
- Use strong and unique passwords for different accounts
- Enable two-factor authentication (2FA)
- Do not share OTPs, passwords, or personal details
- Avoid clicking on suspicious links or emails
- Always verify website authenticity (https://)
- Regularly monitor bank and online accounts
- Avoid using public Wi-Fi for sensitive transactions
- Keep devices and applications updated
